# AQA GCSE English Literature (8702) - Mastery Map

## CORE FOCUS
This syllabus focuses on comprehending, analysing, and evaluating a range of classic literature.

## PAPER 1: Shakespeare and the 19th-Century Novel
- **Section A: Shakespeare**
  - Read, understand, and respond to a chosen play (e.g., Macbeth, Romeo and Juliet).
  - Key skills: detailed textual analysis of an extract, relating the extract to the whole play, understanding contextual factors.
- **Section B: The 19th-Century Novel**
  - Read, understand, and respond to a chosen novel (e.g., A Christmas Carol, Jekyll and Hyde, Jane Eyre).
  - Key skills: detailed textual analysis of an extract, relating the extract to the whole novel, understanding contextual factors.

## PAPER 2: Modern Texts and Poetry
- **Section A: Modern Texts**
  - Answer one essay question from a choice of two on a studied modern prose or drama text (e.g., An Inspector Calls, Lord of the Flies, Blood Brothers).
- **Section B: Poetry Anthology**
  - Compare one named poem with another chosen poem from a studied cluster (e.g., Power and Conflict or Love and Relationships).
- **Section C: Unseen Poetry**
  - Analyse a previously unseen poem, followed by a second question requiring a comparison with a second unseen poem.

## ASSESSMENT OBJECTIVES (AOs)
- **AO1:** Read, understand and respond to texts (maintain critical style, use textual references/quotations).
- **AO2:** Analyse the language, form and structure used by a writer to create meanings and effects.
- **AO3:** Show understanding of the relationships between texts and the contexts in which they were written.
- **AO4:** Use a range of vocabulary and sentence structures for clarity, purpose and effect, with accurate spelling and punctuation.
