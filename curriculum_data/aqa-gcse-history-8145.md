# AQA GCSE History (8145)

## Subject Content
### Paper 1: Understanding the modern world
#### Section A: Period Studies
- **1A America, 1840–1895: Expansion and consolidation**
    - Expansion: Opportunities and challenges in the West.
    - Conflict across America: Causes and consequences of the American Civil War.
    - Consolidation: Forging the nation.
- **1B Germany, 1890–1945: Democracy and dictatorship**
    - Germany and the growth of democracy: Kaiser Wilhelm and the Weimar Republic.
    - Germany and the Depression: The impact of the Depression and the rise of the Nazis.
    - The experiences of Germans under the Nazis: Economic changes, social policy and control.
- **1D America, 1920–1973: Opportunity and inequality**
    - American people and the 'Boom' in the 1920s: Economic boom, social changes, prohibition and organized crime.
    - Americans' experiences of the Depression and New Deal.
    - Post-war America: The post-war economic boom; the Civil Rights Movement.

#### Section B: Wider world depth studies
- **Conflict and tension: The inter-war years, 1918–1939**
    - Peacemaking: The aims of the peacemakers, the Treaty of Versailles.
    - The League of Nations and international peace.
    - The origins and outbreak of the Second World War.
- **Conflict and tension between East and West, 1945–1972**
    - The origins of the Cold War.
    - The development of the Cold War: The arms race, the space race, the Berlin Wall, the Cuban Missile Crisis.
    - Transformation of the Cold War: The Prague Spring, Détente.

### Paper 2: Shaping the nation
#### Section A: Thematic studies
- **Britain: Health and the people: c1000 to the present day**
    - Medicine stands still: Medieval medicine.
    - The beginnings of change: The Renaissance and medicine.
    - A revolution in medicine: The 19th-century development of germ theory, public health.
    - Modern medicine: Penicillin, modern surgery, the NHS.
- **Britain: Power and the people: c1170 to the present day**
    - Defying royal authority: Magna Carta, the Peasants' Revolt.
    - Subversion, rebellion and revolution: The Pilgrimage of Grace, the English Revolution.

#### Section B: British depth studies including the historic environment
- **Norman England, c1066–c1100**
    - The Normans: Conquest and control.
    - Life under the Normans: Feudalism, the Domesday Book.
    - The Norman Church and monasticism.
- **Elizabethan England, c1568–1603**
    - Elizabeth's court and Parliament.
    - Life in Elizabethan times.
    - Troubles at home and abroad: Religious challenges, Mary Queen of Scots, Conflict with Spain.
