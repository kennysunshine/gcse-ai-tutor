# AQA GCSE Geography (8035)

## Subject Content
### Paper 1: Living with the physical environment
#### 3.1.1 The challenge of natural hazards
- Tectonic hazards: Earthquakes and volcanic eruptions. Plate margins, primary and secondary effects, immediate and long-term responses, reducing the risks (monitoring, prediction, protection, planning).
- Weather hazards: Global atmospheric circulation, tropical storms (hurricanes, cyclones, typhoons), extreme weather in the UK.
- Climate change: Evidence for climate change, natural and human causes, mitigating and adapting to climate change.

#### 3.1.2 The living world
- Ecosystems: Interdependence, food webs, impact of change.
- Tropical rainforests: Physical characteristics, interdependence, adaptations, deforestation (causes and impacts), sustainable management.
- Hot deserts: Physical characteristics, interdependence, adaptations, development opportunities and challenges, desertification (causes and reducing the risk).

#### 3.1.3 Physical landscapes in the UK
- Coastal landscapes in the UK: Wave types, coastal processes (weathering, mass movement, erosion, transport, deposition), landforms of coastal erosion and deposition, coastal management (hard and soft engineering).
- River landscapes in the UK: The long profile and cross profile of a river, fluvial processes, landforms of erosion and deposition, river management (hard and soft engineering) and flood risk.

### Paper 2: Challenges in the human environment
#### 3.2.1 Urban issues and challenges
- The global pattern of urban change, urbanization, megacities.
- Urban growth in NEEs/LICs: Social, economic, and environmental opportunities and challenges.
- Urban change in the UK: Distribution of population and major cities. Social, economic, and environmental opportunities and challenges. Urban regeneration.
- Sustainable urban living: Water/energy conservation, waste recycling, green space, transport strategies.

#### 3.2.2 The changing economic world
- Global variations in economic development and quality of life. Indicators of development (GNI, HDI), the Demographic Transition Model.
- Causes and consequences of uneven development. Strategies to reduce the development gap.
- Economic development in NEEs/LICs: The role of TNCs, impacts of international aid, environmental footprints.
- Economic change in the UK: De-industrialization, moving to a post-industrial economy, science and business parks, the north-south divide.

#### 3.2.3 The challenge of resource management
- The global distribution of resources (food, water, energy).
- Provision of food, water and energy in the UK: Growing demand, carbon footprint, water transfer, changing energy mix.
- Energy Resource Management: Global energy demand and supply, impacts of energy insecurity, strategies to increase energy supply (renewables vs non-renewables), sustainable energy use.
