# AQA GCSE Mathematics (8300)

## Subject Content
### 1. Number
- N1: Structure and calculation. Order positive and negative integers, decimals and fractions; use the symbols =, ≠, <, >, ≤, ≥.
- N2: Fractions, decimals and percentages. Apply the four operations, including formal written methods, to integers, decimals and simple fractions.
- N3: Measures and accuracy. Estimate answers; check calculations using approximation and estimation.
- N4: Ratio, proportion and rates of change. Change freely between related standard units (e.g. time, length, area, volume/capacity, mass).

### 2. Algebra
- A1: Notation, vocabulary and manipulation. Use and interpret algebraic manipulation.
- A2: Graphs. Plot graphs of equations that correspond to straight-line graphs in the coordinate plane.
- A3: Solving equations and inequalities. Solve linear equations in one unknown algebraically.
- A4: Sequences. Generate terms of a sequence from either a term-to-term or a position-to-term rule.

### 3. Geometry and Measures
- G1: Properties and constructions. Derive and apply properties and definitions of: special types of quadrilaterals, including square, rectangle, parallelogram, trapezium, kite and rhombus.
- G2: Mensuration and calculation. Use standard units of measure and related concepts (length, area, volume/capacity, mass, time, money, etc.).

### 4. Probability and Statistics
- P1: Probability. Record, describe and analyse the frequency of outcomes of probability experiments using tables and frequency trees.
- P2: Statistics. Record, describe and analyse the frequency of outcomes of probability experiments using tables and frequency trees.

## Tier Rules
- **Foundation Tier:** Grades 1 to 5. Focus on core mathematical concepts and straightforward application.
- **Higher Tier:** Grades 4 to 9. Requires complex multi-step problem solving, advanced algebra, and deeper conceptual understanding.
