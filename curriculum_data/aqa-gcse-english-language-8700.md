# AQA GCSE English Language (8700) - Mastery Map

## CORE FOCUS
This syllabus focuses on reading fluency, critical analysis, and writing for impact.

## PAPER 1: Explorations in Creative Reading and Writing
- **Section A: Reading (Literature Fiction)**
  - Assess understanding of a 20th or 21st-century literary prose fiction text.
  - Key skills: identifying information, analysing language and structure, critical evaluation.
- **Section B: Writing (Descriptive or Narrative)**
  - Write a descriptive or narrative piece.
  - Key skills: crafting compelling texts, varying sentence structures, using advanced vocabulary and punctuation for effect.

## PAPER 2: Writers' Viewpoints and Perspectives
- **Section A: Reading (Non-Fiction and Literary Non-Fiction)**
  - Assess understanding of two linked texts from different time periods (19th century vs 20th/21st century).
  - Key skills: synthesising information from two texts, analysing writers' language and methods, comparing writers' perspectives.
- **Section B: Writing (Writing to Present a Viewpoint)**
  - Write a discursive text (e.g., article, speech, letter).
  - Key skills: structuring an argument, using persuasive techniques, demonstrating a clear and engaging voice.

## SPOKEN LANGUAGE ENDORSEMENT
- Presenting, responding to questions and feedback, and using Standard English. (Separately endorsed, 0% of GCSE grade).

## ASSESSMENT OBJECTIVES (AOs)
- **AO1:** Identify and interpret explicit and implicit information and ideas.
- **AO2:** Explain, comment on and analyse how writers use language and structure to achieve effects.
- **AO3:** Compare writers' ideas and perspectives.
- **AO4:** Evaluate texts critically and support this with appropriate textual references.
- **AO5:** Communicate clearly, effectively and imaginatively.
- **AO6:** Vocabulary, sentence structures, spelling and punctuation.
