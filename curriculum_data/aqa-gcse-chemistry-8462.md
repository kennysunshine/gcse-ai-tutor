# AQA GCSE Chemistry (8462)

## Subject Content
### 1. Atomic Structure and the Periodic Table
- 4.1.1 A simple model of the atom, symbols, relative atomic mass, electronic charge and isotopes.
- 4.1.2 The periodic table: Development of the periodic table, metals and non-metals, Group 0, Group 1, Group 7.

### 2. Bonding, Structure, and the Properties of Matter
- 4.2.1 Chemical bonds, ionic, covalent and metallic.
- 4.2.2 How bonding and structure are related to the properties of substances: States of matter, polymers, alloys.
- 4.2.3 Structure and bonding of carbon: Diamond, graphite, fullerenes and graphene.

### 3. Quantitative Chemistry
- 4.3.1 Chemical measurements, conservation of mass and the quantitative interpretation of chemical equations.
- 4.3.2 Use of amount of substance in relation to masses of pure substances (Higher Tier): Moles, amounts of substances in equations, limiting reactants.
- 4.3.3 Yield and atom economy of chemical reactions (Higher Tier): Percentage yield, atom economy.

### 4. Chemical Changes
- 4.4.1 Reactivity of metals: Metal oxides, The reactivity series, Extraction of metals and reduction.
- 4.4.2 Reactions of acids: Reactions of acids with metals, Neutralisation of acids and salt production, The pH scale and neutralisation.
- 4.4.3 Electrolysis: The process of electrolysis, Electrolysis of molten ionic compounds, Using electrolysis to extract metals.

## Required Practical Activities
- Making Salts: Preparation of a pure, dry sample of a soluble salt from an insoluble oxide or carbonate, using a Bunsen burner to heat dilute acid.
- Electrolysis: Investigate what happens when aqueous solutions are electrolysed using inert electrodes.
- Rates of Reaction: Investigate how changes in concentration affect the rates of reactions by a method involving measuring the volume of a gas produced and a method involving a change in colour or turbidity.
